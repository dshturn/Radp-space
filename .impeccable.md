## Design Context

### Users
Contractor companies operating in the oil & gas sector. Two distinct audiences: **office coordinators** who manage documents and personnel records at a desk, and **on-site supervisors** who check readiness status from a tablet or phone in an industrial environment (rig, field site). Both use the app regularly; mobile usage is significant. Tasks are compliance-driven — uploading certs, checking expiry status, approving personnel for jobs. The stakes are high (regulatory compliance, safety), so errors feel costly.

### Brand Personality
**Measured, authoritative, reliable.** This is a compliance tool used in safety-critical contexts. It should feel like a system you trust under pressure — not flashy, not bureaucratic-ugly. Think: the instrument panel on a drilling control room, a well-structured technical report, a high-quality work permit system. The interface should inspire confidence and reduce cognitive load.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark only. Oil & gas operations run at all hours; dark UIs reduce eye strain in mixed indoor/outdoor lighting. Current navy/amber palette is appropriate and distinctive — amber signals alerts and action effectively against dark backgrounds.
- **Visual tone**: Utilitarian precision. Dense but readable. Hierarchy through weight and spacing, not decoration. Status communication is the core design job — expired/valid/missing states must be immediately scannable.
- **Anti-reference**: Avoid anything that looks like a SaaS marketing dashboard (hero stats, gradient fills, cards with icons+headings repeated endlessly). This is a field tool, not a product showcase.

### Design Principles
1. **Status first**: Every piece of data has a compliance state. The design's primary job is making those states instantly readable — green/amber/red must be unambiguous at a glance.
2. **Dense but breathable**: Field professionals don't have time to scroll through spacious UIs. Pack information tightly but use whitespace deliberately to separate functional zones.
3. **Mobile is real**: On-site users are on phones or tablets. Touch targets, readable font sizes, and one-handed operation patterns are non-negotiable.
4. **Trust through consistency**: Every button, badge, and state indicator should behave the same way throughout. Inconsistency erodes confidence in a compliance tool.
5. **No decorative noise**: Every visual element should do a job. If it doesn't communicate status, hierarchy, or action — it shouldn't be there.
